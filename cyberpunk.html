What is 3D Scanning?

3D scanning is the process of capturing the shape and appearance of real-world objects as a digital, three-dimensional model. Highly-detailed 3D models provide accurate measurements, dimensions, and mounting points, freeing you from being tethered to the physical item you're designing around.

Working with 3D models can speed up product design and prototyping cycles, resulting in more opportunities for quality control, lower development costs, and better finished products.

What is 3D Scanning Used For?

Custom 3D scanning technology has exciting applications across many industries, such as manufacturing, automotive, industrial design, and more.

How Does 3D Scanning Work?

Using the latest in handheld laser scanning technology, we bounce laser beams off an item's surface to analyze its features and generate a detailed, three-dimensional model, which we save as an STL file.

The STL file can be used in the software program of your choice. You can use your 3D model for a variety of purposes, ranging from 3D printing prototypes, modeling designs, or running simulations.

Our precision 3D laser scanning can capture intricate systems or single pieces. Our models are accurate up to .02 thousandths on parts as small as a bolt, or as large as a vehicle.
